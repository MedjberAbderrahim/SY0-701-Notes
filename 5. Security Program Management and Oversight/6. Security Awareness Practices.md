# Phishing Campaigns
Organize automated phishing campaigns, i.e. sending fraudulent mails and simulating real phishing mails with the objective of:
- Testing and improving employees' recognition for phishing through its indicators, e.g. spelling and grammatical errors, domain name and email inconsistencies, unusual attachments, ...
- Testing the implemented phishing filters
- Testing the employees' ability to not fall into phishing, and reporting performance (how good they report phishing)
Such tests can be conducted easily, and used as basis to evaluate a company's security awareness posture and to direct future awareness training.
# Anomalous Behavior Recognition
Also look into any peculiar behavior, such as:
- **Risky Behavior**: Modifying hosts or core OS files, uploading sensitive information, ...
- **Unexpected Behavior**: Logon from another country, increase in data transfers, ...
- **Unintentional Behavior**: Typo in used domain name, misplacing USB drives, misconfiguration security settings, ...
# Reporting and Monitoring
Security awareness monitoring & report generation must be automated, tracking awareness metrics (password manager adoption, phishing clicks, ...)
It can be:
- **Initial**: First occurrence of event, e.g. user falls into phishing, poses good opportunity for training.
- **Recurring**: Helps identifies high frequency security issues, and to direct extended training for specific users.
# Development
The **Security Awareness Team** creates and manages the training of users, e.g. establishing minimum awareness level, designing compliance-specific training, define the metrics used later in execution
# Execution
The **Security Awareness Team** creates training content (slides, ...), and documents the awareness posture and process for the stakeholders
> <b><u>Security Awareness Training</u></b>: Employees and anyone with access to our devices/networks must be trained before given that access, training must be also be tracked and recorded.
# User Guidance and Training
## Policy/Handbooks
Company's policies must be recorded and be available online on the company's intranet for employees, they should also be included/referenced in the employee's playbook.
## Situational Awareness
Users must know how to detect attacks and threats, e.g. phishing emails, unknown USB drives, ...
## Insider Threat
Must harden systems and correctly setup permissions to avoid insider threats, which are particularly hard to defend against.
## Password Management
Must correctly setup password policies, generally through centralized administration platforms (group policies, active directory, ...)
## Removable Media and Cables
Training must also cover physical media and USBs, as they are a widely used attack vector
## Social Engineering
Users must be trained to recognize, stop, not fall to, and report social engineering, to which they are a prime target to.
## Operational Security
Raise awareness about data's importance and why it should be protected, by clarifying how it can be used by hackers.
## Hybrid/Remote Work Environments
Raising awareness of working from home risks, e.g. family touching what they shouldn't, insecure remote work environment, ...
