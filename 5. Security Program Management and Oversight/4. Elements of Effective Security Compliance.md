# Compliance
- Compliance is the process of meeting a series of local or international standards or agreements.
- The amount of compliance required depends on the type of business and area of work
- Lack of compliance is penalized with fines, loss of employment, jail, ...
# Compliance Reporting
## Internal
Ensures the organization's conformity to requirements (state, local, federal, ...), in large orgs it is managed by a **CCO (Central Compliance Officer)**.
## External
The case when compliance is required/demanded by a 3<sup>rd</sup> party, can also require regular/on-going reporting, with penalties in case of missing them.
# Consequences of Non-Compliance
## Fines / Sanctions
<b><u>Example</u>:</b> **HIPAA (The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)** non-compliance penalties range from to over million per violation (adjusted for inflation), depending on culpability, with annual caps reaching up to million. Violations can result in civil fines, criminal penalties (including up to 10 years in jail), and mandatory corrective action plans.
## Reputational Damage
Can lead to short term drop of stock prices or loss of customers
> <b><u>Example</u>:</b> Uber was fined $148 million for covering up for a breach for 1 year and allegedly paying hackers $100,000 and had them sign an **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)** to not leak it; and its Chief Security Officer was jailed later.
## Loss of License
Significant economic sanction, can stop the company from selling its product or other companies from buying from that company.
## Contractual Impacts
Contracts may require a minimum compliance level which is legally binding and can lead to court (not always)
# Compliance Monitoring
## Due Diligence/Care
A way to show that the company is serious about terms of compliance, due care tends to refer to internal activities while due diligence is often associated with 3<sup>rd</sup> party activities.
## Attestation and Acknowledgement
The person managing the compliance signs the attestation of good compliance and is responsible that information is done in good faith.
## Internal and External
Must always monitor both internal and external if applicable using tools and monitoring of 3<sup>rd</sup> party operations.
## Automation
The extensive amount of compliance monitoring pushes and enforces usage of automation, especially in large organizations, such tools compile reports, data, ...; to ensure compliance is met.
# Privacy
Companies must follow privacy laws to correctly manage the huge amounts of user data they generate/store
## Legal Implications
### Local/Regional
Privacy limits set by state & local governments, e.g. legal information, vehicle registration details, ...
### National
Privacy laws for everyone in the country, e.g. **HIPAA (The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)**, ...
### Global
Privacy laws that concern multiple countries.
> <b><u>Example</u>:</b> The [GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)](https://gdpr-info.eu/) is the European Union's comprehensive data privacy law, in effect since May 25, 2018, designed to protect individuals' personal data and privacy. It applies to organizations within the EEA and those outside if they process data of EU residents. Key aspects include strict consent rules, data subject rights (access, erasure), and fines up to €20 million or 4% of global turnover.
## Right to Be Forgotten
The right to demand complete erasure of private data from a website
## Data Subject
**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)** defines a data subject is a living, identified, or identifiable natural person whose personal data (name, ID number, address, ...) is processed by a controller or processor. They are the individuals (customers, employees, users) to whom data relates, granting them specific rights to access, rectify, erase, and control their personal information.
This puts privacy from the perspective of an individual instead of a company.
## Data Roles
- Already covered in 5.1
- Data owner, controller, processor, ...
## Data Inventory and Retention
Listing of all data stored by a company with other details (owner, update frequency, ...), can be used for internal purposes (project collaboration, IT security, ...) or external purposes (with consideration to laws and regulations)
