# Vendor Assessment
Specify in contracts with partner 3<sup>rd</sup> party risk analysis steps to ensure our data is well-handled
## Penetration Testing
- Simulating an attack while exploiting vulnerabilities found, can be conducted locally or by a specialized, hired 3<sup>rd</sup> party.
- Often mandated by compliance rules, or signed in a contract with a 3<sup>rd</sup> party
- All penetration tests must follow a pre-signed **rules of engagement** document, specifying scope, time, place, ...
## Right-to-Audit
Requires the contracted company to audit regularly and in certain ways, in order to ensure the contractor's data, functions, or traffic is safe
## Evidence of Internal Audits
Audits are generally performed by 3<sup>rd</sup> party auditors neutral to the contract between the contractor and the vendor, to evaluate/improve implemented security controls, a good idea to regularly audit (not single time) even without compliance requirements.
## Supply Chain Analysis
- Operations with external entities involved in creating the product must be analyzed and secured, e.g. organizations, people, activities, ...
- Analysis involves coordination between groups, possible areas of improvement, and documenting business process changes
## Independent Assessments
3<sup>rd</sup> party assessments are useful because they provide neutral evaluation, also helps in widening horizons, they're also specialists with solid experience and knowledge, therefore valuable insights
# Vendor Selection
## Due Diligence
Must thoroughly verify the vendor before signing, from information verification to background checks and personnel interviews
## Conflict of Interest
Sometimes potential interests could compromise judgment and sabotage the business relation, e.g. vendor also works with largest competitor, mentioning of "gifts" in the contract, ...; These problems may hinder business between the 2 parties in the future.
# Vendor Monitoring
- Continuous management of vendor relationship through continuous, regular monitoring, e.g. financial health checks, IT security reviews, news articles on the vendor, ...
- Different vendors generally require different degree and type of monitoring, good practice to assign a person or a team to manage the relationship with a certain vendor and do monitoring
# Questionnaires
Update/Adapt the vendor risk analysis using the vendor's answers to sent questionnaires, which are a good way to directly get answers from the vendor for security-related questions, e.g. vendor's **due diligence** process, disaster recovery plans, implemented secure storage methods, ...
# Agreement Types
## SLA (Service Level Agreement)
Generally used between customers and service providers to set minimum terms for the provided service, maximum downtime, response time agreement, technical support, ...
## MOU (Memorandum of Understanding) vs MOA (Memorandum of Agreement)
A **MOU (Memorandum of Understanding)** is a non-binding document outlining a general agreement or intent to collaborate with some details, often viewed as a "formal handshake". A **MOA (Memorandum of Agreement)** is a more specific, usually binding contract that details responsibilities, deliverables, and, often, the exchange of funds or resources; but it is still less formal and legal than a final contract.
## MSA (Master Service Agreement)
A MSA (Master Service Agreement) is a **foundational** contract establishing agreed-upon terms, such as payment, liability, and intellectual property, for an ongoing business relationship. By setting these ground rules upfront, it sets a foundation for future projects
## WO (Work Order) / SOW (Statement of Work)
- Specific, detailed list of provided services, builds upon the MSA as foundation
- Defines in detail what the expectations are, used to evaluate the work after it's been done
## NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
- A formal contract specifying the details that must be kept confidential thorough the relationship, e.g. trade secrets, business activities, ...; must be signed before actually discussing those details.
- Can be unilateral (one way), or bilateral/multilateral (+2 ways).
## BPA (Business Partners Agreement)
A legally binding document outlining the roles, ownership details, responsibilities, profit/loss sharing, and dispute resolution methods for partners
