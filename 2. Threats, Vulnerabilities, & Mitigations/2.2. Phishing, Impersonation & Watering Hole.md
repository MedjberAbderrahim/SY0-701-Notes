## Phishing and Social Engineering Attacks

Phishing tricks people into believing fake messages are real to steal sensitive info like passwords.
Common methods: email, SMS, messaging apps.
Always verify messages from trusted sources.
### Signs of Phishing
-  Suspicious links
-  Poor formatting
-  Fake sender addresses
-  Urgent language
-  Requests for private info
### Case Study: Fake Rackspace Login
Clicking a phishing link led to a fake login page with visual differences and branded images to steal credentials.
### Risks of Clicking Links
Risks include credential theft, malware, unauthorized access, and spreading scams.
### Common Techniques
-  **Spoofing:** Fake email addresses
-  **Typosquatting:** Fake domains similar to real ones
-  **Pretexting:** Fake stories to lure info
-  **Vishing:** Fake phone calls
-  **Smishing:** Malicious SMS
### Examples
-  Fake security alerts or account updates
-  Messages from delivery services with fake links
-  Bank calls requesting info
-  Texts asking for verification codes
### Protection Tips
-  Avoid clicking links in unsolicited messages
-  Verify sender details
-  Hover over links to check URLs
-  Watch for formatting issues
-  Use multi-factor authentication
-  Keep software updated
-  Learn common scams

## Phone Call Impersonation & Fraud Prevention

Typical scam calls involve impersonation or fraud, often from YouTube recordings.
### Examples of Scam Calls
-  **Fake Microsoft Support:** Claims to be Wendy from Microsoft with urgent computer issues. Look for false identity and urgency.
-  **US Treasury Notice:** Claims to be from the Treasury about enforcement actions. Poor grammar indicates a scam.
-  **Credit Card Offer:** Congratulates on payment history and offers 0% interest—aims to steal financial info.
### Impersonation Tactics
Attackers pose as trusted figures to steal info, using:
-  Fake help desk or IT staff
-  High-ranking officials like VPs or finance managers
-  Technical jargon to seem legitimate
The goal is to make victims comfortable and less suspicious.
### Goals of Impersonation
Attackers aim to:
1. **Steal Sensitive Data:** Credit card, SSN, bank info
2. **Facilitate Identity Fraud:** Open accounts or commit fraud with stolen info
### Types of Identity Fraud
Impersonation can lead to:
-  **Account Opening:** Fake accounts, mail sent to attackers
-  **Loan Fraud:** Using stolen identity for illegal loans
-  **Benefits Fraud:** Filing false tax returns or claiming benefits, harming finances and credit
### Prevention Tips
-  **Don’t Share Personal Info:** Never give passwords or SSNs without verification
-  **Verify Callers:** Ask for callback info or verify through official channels
-  **Be Suspicious of Unsolicited Requests:** Treat unexpected requests cautiously
-  **Follow Protocols:** Use proper procedures to verify identities

## Watering Hole

A cyber-attack where criminals infect a legitimate, frequently visited website (the "watering hole") to compromise users who go there, waiting for them to get infected with malware to steal data or access corporate networks, much like a predator waits at a water source.
Attackers research a target group, compromise a site they trust (e.g., an industry forum, government site, or social media), and inject malicious code to silently infect visitors' devices, often using zero-day exploits or drive-by downloads
