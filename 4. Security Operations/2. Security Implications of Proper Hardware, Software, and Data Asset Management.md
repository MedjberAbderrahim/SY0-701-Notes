## Acquisition/Procurement Process
- Purchasing is a multi-step process, starts with a request from the user, then negotiation with the supplier, then the purchase action, followed by an invoice, payment, ...
- It involves **budget analysis** and formal **sign-offs** from IT, management, and the purchasing department.
- Negotiations cover pricing, **licensing terms**, and contract details.
- Invoices include specific **payment terms**, such as immediate payment or a 30-to-60-day window.
## Assignment/Accounting
- Track tangible goods in a **central asset tracking system** for tracking & management purposes.
### Ownership
- Associate a person to that asset, useful for tracking the asset and knowing who to contact for updates.
### Classification
- Classify asset by type (laptop, mobile, etc.) and whether it is **hardware (capital expenditure)** or **software (operating expenditure)**.
- Hardware typically **depreciates** over time, affecting tax liability, whereas software generally does not.
## Monitoring/Asset Tracking
### Inventory
- Maintain a list of all devices on the network, such as **laptops, routers, and cables**.
- Inventory data assists the **help desk** by populating tickets with the specific make and model of a user's device.
- **Enumeration** allows the system to track a device as a single entity or as its individual components, like CPU, memory, and storage.
- **Asset Tags** (physical labels or barcodes) are attached to provide identification and act as a **security feature** if the device is lost or stolen.
## Disposal and Sanitisaton
- **Media Sanitisation**: When reusing a device, sensitive data must be removed using utilities that **securely delete** information so it cannot be recovered.
- **Physical Destruction**: To guarantee data is gone, drives can be **shredded, pulverised, drilled, or incinerated**.
- **Degaussing**: This uses a strong **electromagnetic field** to delete data and render hard drives unusable.
- **Third-Party Destruction**: For large quantities of devices, organisations often hire specialists who must provide a **certificate of destruction** as confirmation.
## Data Retention
- **Mandates**: Some organisations are legally required to keep specific data, such as **emails or financial records**, for a set number of years.
- **Policies**: Retention procedures should be part of a **backup and disaster recovery** strategy to ensure data can be restored if accidentally deleted.
- **Requirements**: Different types of data have different **retention lengths** and procedural requirements.
